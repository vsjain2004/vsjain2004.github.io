NN Acceleration for Pervasive Extreme Edge Intelligence - Oct 2024 to May 2025

Honors Capstone Project

For my Honors Capstone project, I worked in Iowa State University's Duwe Lab under the guidance of Dr. Henry Duwe and Ph.D. student Rohit Sahu to design and deploy quantized neural networks on the MSP430FR5994 microcontroller for CIFAR-10 image classification under strict energy and memory constraints.

Project Details

In this project, we used a Neural Architecture Search approach to develop three neural network architectures optimized for the resource constraints of the MSP430 microcontroller. The models were trained in TensorFlow and quantized to 8-bit integers using TensorFlow Lite. After verification on a modified TensorFlow Lite for Microcontrollers (TFLite-Micro) platform on PC, the networks were deployed on the MSP430 and evaluated for latency and energy efficiency. I extended the TFLite-Micro framework by implementing additional operators required for efficient inference on this platform. The project was presented as a poster at Iowa State University's Honors Poster Presentation in Spring 2025. The poster can be viewed here.

Skills and Knowledge Gained

This project strengthened my understanding of embedded machine learning and resource-constrained inference. I gained hands-on experience with quantization, microcontroller deployment, and the TFLite/TFLite-Micro frameworks, including extending their functionality to support custom network operations.
