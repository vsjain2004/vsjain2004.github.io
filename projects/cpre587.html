MAC Design Optimization for CprE 5870 - Fall 2024

Course Project | Hardware Design for Machine Learning

CprE 5870 provides a comprehensive introduction to hardware architectures for machine learning: covering everything from machine learning frameworks to spatial architectures and hardware design optimizations. For the course project, my team designed multiple Multiply-Accumulate (MAC) units and evaluated them based on their performance and power efficiency.

Project Details

For this project, my team and I implemented MAC units in VHDL using two distinct multiplier architectures and two pipeline configurations to perform quantized 8-bit and 4/6-bit multiply-accumulate operations. We explored two synthesis and three implementation strategies in Vivado to identify the most efficient combination. The designs were evaluated across five metrics, including area, power, and various throughput-to-power efficiency ratios. While the codebase is currently unavailable, a detailed project report can be viewed here.

Skills and Knowledge Gained

This project strengthened my understanding of digital hardware design and optimization. I gained practical experience in implementing and comparing multiplier architectures, tuning synthesis and implementation strategies in Vivado, and evaluating FPGA performance on a Zynq-7000 SoC. Through this process, I developed a deeper appreciation for the trade-offs between area, power, and performance in hardware acceleration.
