Optical Recognition in PCBs for CprE 5750 - Spring 2023

Course Project | Computational Perception

CprE 5750 focuses on image and signal processing techniques, covering topics such as filtering, segmentation, and feature extraction. For the course project, my team developed a program to reverse-engineer 1- and 2-layer Printed Circuit Boards (PCBs) into their corresponding schematics.

Project Details

Our team designed a program that processes user-provided PCB images to automatically extract circuit connections and component information. The workflow integrated a convolutional neural network (CNN), an OCR model, and several handcrafted image filters to identify traces, vias, and silkscreen labels. My primary role was evaluating multiple OCR models and optimizing their use to accurately read PCB silkscreen text. The project was presented as part of the course's final evaluation, and the report is available here.

Skills and Knowledge Gained

This project provided hands-on experience in image preprocessing, OCR-based text extraction, and the integration of neural networks within a computer vision pipeline. I also gained practical skills in combining deep learning with traditional image processing techniques to solve complex engineering problems, as well as in model evaluation and performance optimization for real-world datasets.
